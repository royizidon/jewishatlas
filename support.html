Jewish Atlas

Preserving Jewish Sites. Safeguarding Jewish Continuity.

Major Support

Jewish history is carried not only in texts, but in places — synagogues, cemeteries, neighborhoods, schools, and communal institutions across the world. Many of these sites are disappearing.

Jewish Atlas exists to ensure that Jewish sites, memory, and presence remain visible, discoverable, and preserved — with the seriousness, accuracy, and durability this responsibility demands.

The Moment We Are In

Across continents, Jewish heritage sites face neglect, conflict, urban development, and demographic change. In parallel, rising antisemitism and global instability have made Jewish continuity increasingly dependent on connection, visibility, and reliable knowledge.

When sites disappear without record, entire chapters of Jewish life are lost permanently — not only for historians, but for families, communities, and future generations.

This is a long-horizon obligation. Jewish Atlas is built as permanent cultural infrastructure — a resource designed to endure.

What Jewish Atlas Provides

Jewish Atlas is a global, living map of Jewish sites — past and present — consolidated into a single authoritative platform. It integrates verified records, geographic precision, and structured knowledge to serve preservation, education, and community connection.

Documentation & Preservation Recording sites, institutions, and historical footprints with durability and care.
Verification & Historical Rigor Cross-referencing community input with archival sources and field validation.
Accessibility & Continuity Making Jewish sites discoverable for families, educators, and communities worldwide.
Long-Term Infrastructure Building a platform designed for permanence, stewardship, and responsible growth.

Stewardship & Integrity

Jewish Atlas is built with an emphasis on accuracy, provenance, and long-term custodianship. Our approach balances community contributions with research standards appropriate for permanent cultural infrastructure.

Verification Standards Triangulating community submissions with archival sources and field validation where feasible.
Data Governance Structured curation, versioning practices, and documented edits to protect integrity over time.
Preservation & Continuity Redundancy, backups, and a custodianship plan designed for long-term reliability.
Responsible Growth Expanding coverage without compromising rigor, clarity, or stewardship obligations.

Why This Work Matters

Preservation is not only about memory. It is about continuity — ensuring that Jewish presence remains visible, legible, and accessible, even when physical sites are vulnerable.

Jewish Atlas transforms fragmented and fragile knowledge into a durable global resource — a foundation for future scholarship, education, and community connection.
