Jewish communities have existed across the world for centuries. Synagogues, cemeteries, neighborhoods, schools, and everyday places leave physical traces — many of them fragile.

Jewish Atlas exists to make these places visible, remembered, and connected — before they disappear.

What is Jewish Atlas?

Jewish Atlas is a public, community-built geospatial map of Jewish heritage and living Jewish spaces worldwide.

Explore the map. Add places you know. Help preserve what still remains.

What you’ll find

Not just categories — real places across the world:

  • A small synagogue in Dalas.
  • A cemetery in Kraków.
  • A Jewish neighborhood in Fez.
  • Kosher delis in New York.
  • Living communities in London.

If something is missing or inaccurate, your correction matters.
